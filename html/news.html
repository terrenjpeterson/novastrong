Expansion Update: Plans Moving Ahead

April 3, 2015


Dear NOVA Family and Friends,

We’re pleased to report that our expansion plans are proceeding on all fronts! The many folks involved in the project continue to be excited by what this opportunity means for the NOVA community. With the addition of more water, all of our swimmers will benefit – whether through better practice times, less crowded lanes, more flexible swim lessons or dedicated long course training space. Parents will benefit too, with reduced travel and more comfortable home meets. In addition, more space will allow NOVA to increase the breadth of our offerings, introduce new programming, and add to our outreach programs. Our impact will be dramatically amplified by increasing our capacity.

Design & Construction Plans Moving Ahead

Much is being done to prepare for building the new facility. The Board is working closely with Moseley Architects to submit our Plan of Development (POD) to the county next week. The POD includes the site plan and the building concept. The process of approval for the POD is five to six months; during this time, no site work can be done; however, we will continue to proceed with design work for the building and pool during this time.

The Board of Directors has already authorized Moseley to complete the next set of designs on the project. Once that is complete, the Board will reevaluate the budget based on our capital campaign results and make a decision on whether to move forward with the rest of the design work, which includes finalizing the design, creating the construction documents and selecting a contractor for the project.

Fundraising Continues

Our total for the capital campaign now stands at nearly $600,000, over a quarter of the project fundraising goal of $2 million. While this is a great start, there is a long way to go to realize our dream of a 50 meter pool! The project requires the support of all NOVA families to succeed, and we hope that we can count on yours.

We are excited to enter into the next phase of our capital campaign. We are in the process of organizing group socials, many to be hosted personally by NOVA families, where we will be able to explain the details of our vision in a fun and relaxed setting. These gatherings will begin in late April, and every NOVA family will receive an invitation. Guest lists are based on summer swim team/address (to the best of our ability!). At each gathering you’ll hear more about the status and timing of the project, and be able to ask questions and talk to board members and coaching staff about the project. We hope that you will attend as it will give you a much better understanding of the project, why the project is important to our children and our community, and the crucial role that each of us plays in making the project a reality. We can’t wait to share our enthusiasm about NOVA’s bright future!

What's Next?

While we are anxious to break ground, this won’t happen without the financial support of our NOVA families and friends. Our hope is that you will consider making a gift to support the project. NOVA is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, so gifts are tax deductible to the extent allowed by law. Additionally, donors can choose to spread their pledges out over up to five years. As an individual or corporate donor, you’ll receive various benefits for making a gift at certain levels, including waivers for fundraising and/or volunteer hours, preferred parking, and more. Contributions made by May 31, 2015 will be recognized as part of our Founders’ Club. If you want to learn more, feel free to reach out to our Capital Campaign Steering Committee: Turner Broughton, Faith Kallman, Andrea Wassmer or me. To make a gift now, please contact Ann McGee, NOVA’s Development Manager, at 804.750.1183 or annmcgee@novaswim.org.

Expansion Update: Official Kickoff

February 25, 2015


Dear NOVA Family and Friends,

First of all, thank you for choosing NOVA. We appreciate your being part of the NOVA family and for your commitment to swimming, whether it is through our competitive swim team, swim lessons, or adult programs.

Founded in 1987 with ninety swimmers utilizing rented pool space across the Richmond region, NOVA now offers a full-service aquatics program with close to 800 competitive swimmers, a lessons program that teaches some 5,000 lessons per year, a nationally-recognized Masters team, various programs to meet the needs of recreational swimmers, and the only local, full-service swim shop in the region.

NOVA’s development over time has allowed it to broaden its programs and fulfill its mission – to develop excellence in swimmers of all ages and abilities by challenging and motivating them to reach their full potential in swimming AND in life. This progress has always been triggered by the expansion of our facilities. As we have increased capacity, we have expanded our offerings and our impact in the community.

The dedication and commitment of the families, coaches and swimmers that came before us have always driven NOVA’s growth. Prior to 1997, NOVA practiced at three widely scattered facilities across Richmond. In 1997, NOVA’s families and coaches built NOVA its own facility, bringing our swimmers together under one roof. Less than ten years later the NOVA community pulled together once again when it became clear that we had outgrown our original pool, building our second pool in 2005.

Just as we were during those two critical periods, our current facility limits our ability to execute our mission. For the competitive team, this means crowded lanes, less than ideal practice times, and burdensome and expensive travel for our families. For our lessons program, we are turning interested swimmers away due to a lack of space. For our community, we are unable to offer extensive after school learn-to-swim programs for underprivileged children in our area, or to serve other groups, such as elderly clientele who would benefit from daily lap swimming or exercise programs.

We are proud of how far NOVA has come and the success of our club. Our competitive team is currently ranked in the top ten nationally; and we consistently send swimmers to the Olympic Trials and to elite university swim programs. More importantly, NOVA offers our swimmers a positive, healthy experience that fosters lifelong friendships and teaches important life lessons.

We have the opportunity to achieve even more: we have the focus, coaches and family support to get to a new level of service and impact. To achieve our goals, NOVA plans to construct a new 50-meter indoor pool on our current campus. We have hired Moseley Architects to design and help construct this important project. NOVA swimmers will be able to train in an Olympic-sized pool, and we will be able to offer additional lane space and practice times to all training groups. The addition of the 50-meter pool will enable NOVA to raise the temperature of the current competition pool to better facilitate swim lessons, introduce older adult wellness programs, and seek out sports medicine and rehabilitation partnerships with local organizations.

As in the past, we count on the financial support of NOVA’s families and friends, as well as those businesses and organizations that support our mission. Funding for the new pool will come from three sources: (1) cash on hand; (2) borrowing; and, (3) individual and corporate donations. This is where you have a role to play.

We would like to have the new pool open by the start of the short course season in the fall of 2016. However, we can only break ground if NOVA’s families are truly devoted to the project. To determine whether such a commitment exists, we began meeting with some NOVA families around the first of the year. In just 45 days, the families with whom we have met affirmed their commitment to make NOVA’s latest dream a reality, pledging over $525,000 toward the construction of a new pool, with most donations coming in at the $15,000 and $25,000 levels!

While this is an outstanding start, NOVA needs to raise $2,000,000 in individual and corporate contributions to move forward with the proposed expansion. We are confident that we can meet our goal, but doing so means that it is going to take all of us to make the new pool a reality. We have yet to speak to the overwhelming majority of NOVA families about the project, and we are anxious to do so over the next two months. In order to help tell the story we intend to hold parent education socials to provide details and answer questions, and to continue meeting with our many interested families. We will also be releasing a website in support of the 2015 campaign effort very soon.

Perhaps most importantly, we need to continue pressing forward towards our campaign goal. To that end, we are inviting all NOVA families to help us reach our fundraising target of $2,000,000. NOVA is a 501(c)(3) organization so contributions are tax deductible, and they can be spread out for up to a five-year period. The Board has approved the benefits that individuals will receive for their charitable contribution, including such things as a reserved parking space for meets, volunteer hour waivers, and private lessons with a NOVA coach.

All contributors who make their pledge by April 30, 2015 will be recognized as part of NOVA’s Founders Club – those individuals and businesses that helped launch the project, and made it possible to pursue this dream of more water for our swimmers and our community.

Swimming changes lives. Stay tuned for more details about how you can help.
