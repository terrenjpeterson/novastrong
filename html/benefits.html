Our Mission

A NOVA for the future... The Capital Expansion Project.

Expanding the NOVA facility will benefit all of NOVA's current programming.

For the Competitive Youth Swim Team...

More space will have an immediate impact on our competitive youth team. NOVA is the only Top Twenty club in the country that does not have access to a 50M training facility. Having a long-course pool will allow our swimmers to train more effectively for the long-course season. NOVA will be able to host long-course meets, relieving burdensome and expensive travel for our families.

Perhaps more importantly, more lanes will mean less crowded practices for all team swimmers. We will be able to evaluate practice times and shift them to be more ideal for our families. We expect to be able to offer benefits to each of our training levels. Please note that the potential improvements listed below are subject to change.

Novice Groups

  • Offer an additional practice time at 4:30pm for all Novice groups; expand our 5:30pm practice time to offer an additional time for Novice Prep; offer four days of 5:30pm for Novice Silver and Novice Gold
    •
  • Eliminate shifting from one pool to the other during the middle of practices.
    •
  • Allow for "end lane" coaching, increasing coaching vision/coverage and offering more personalized instruction.
    •

    Age Groups

  • Alleviate lane overcrowding during some practices, particularly at 4:00pm in AGDS and AGDG, and at 5:30 with AGP/AGS.
  • Allow for lower swimmer to coach ratios.
  • Offer a later practice time for AGDS/AGDG to attract swimmers who currently do not have workable practice options.
  • Provide a consistent number of lanes for each group to ensure that lane shifting does not impact practice efficiencies.
  • Return AGP/AGS/AGG to earlier Friday practice times.
  • Increase retention in these groups.

    • Senior Groups

  • Allow for sufficient lane space for SDS to accomodate varied skill levels within group.
  • Institute more attractive practice time (return to 6:30pm and expand Friday option) for SDS.
  • Reduce the number of swimmers per lane, allowing for better training outcomes.
  • Offer a more effective, more specialized practice environment for elite groups.
  • Eliminate current caps on number of swimmers moving into SDG PM due to space constraints.
  • Allow for group moves among elite training groups at any time of the year.
  • Allow for growth in most elite training groups.
  • Offer more flexibility for alumni returning to practice over breaks and summer.
  • Offer specialty training (sprints, mid-distance, stroke diagnostics) with increase in lane space.
  • Eliminate signups for senior doubles; increase schedule flexibility for most committed swimmers.

    • All Team Members

  • Reduce practice changes or cancellations when NOVA is hosting home meets.
  • Allow for year-round long course training for elite swimmer and April-August long course training for all levels.
  • Shorten meet lengths by running heats in two pools.
  • Show meet results on new multi-line, multi-pool scoreboard.
  • Offer in-house dry land training space, allowing for strength and conditioning training for more practice groups and reducing dependence on outside training space.
  • Host 2-3 long course meets at home, saving families hundreds of dollars in travel costs and time missed from work/school.
  • Increase retention and customer satisfaction at all levels due to more options, flexibility and offerings.
  • Expand coaching staff to meet increased needs.

    • For Swimnova Lessons...

    for the lessons program, NOVA will be able to offer more after school and evening options, making it more convenient for lessons families. Families will have more classes from which to choose, and we will have less families who do not get their first choice of day/time. Some possible benefits of expansion are listed here.

  • Convert current competition pool to a "warm water" pool, allowing for better experiences for lessons participants (and instructors) and greater lessons retention.
  • Expand weekday lessons to prime time of 4:00 to 7:00pm, opening up options for families with two working parents.
  • Expand number of classes taught during 2:30 to 4:00pm timeframe, allowing families with young team members and lessons participants to bring swimmers at same time.
  • Expand class options to allow for more space in each level and facilitate progression through the program.
  • Reduce dependencies on weekend lessons, which are often forced to skip weeks due to home meets, or be able to schedule lessons again during meets.
  • Offer more employment opportunities for older swimmers to teach lessons.
