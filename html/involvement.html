A NOVA for the Future

Community Involvement

NOVA is a nonprofit organization that has always had a strong commitment to the community. We have a long history of giving to others through our club. As our facility expansion proceeds, we intend to expand our current outreach programming and expand to serve new groups.

Bon Secours movin' mania Partnership

In 2013, NOVA established a partnership with Bon Secours to bring its movin’ mania program to the West End of Richmond for the first time. The movin’ mania program, designed to teach at-risk kids skills for healthy living, partnered with NOVA to offer free swim lessons to children who otherwise would not be able to learn to swim. 35 children participated in the first summer, and some of them have continued taking lessons throughout the year. We will continue this program during the summer of 2015 as well.

Ruby F. Carver Elementary School Partnership

Ruby F. Carver Elementary School is located less than two miles from NOVA. NOVA is an official partner of this school and initially supported it by offering spots for students in movin’ mania. We have also supported their PTA and administration with grants and offered volunteer help for school events. In coming years, NOVA will be deepening our relationship with the school to support it in other ways.

Dunk Hunger initiative to support FeedMore

Since 2012, The Virginia Swim Shop (wholly owned by NOVA) has partnered with FeedMore and Richmond Family Magazine to run Dunk Hunger, a summer food and funds drive which encourages summer league swim teams to collect donations for the food bank. Over 75,000 meals have been donated through these efforts. As a presenting partner, NOVA helps organize the event, underwrite the costs and manage marketing efforts.

Goochland Christmas Mother

For more than a decade, NOVA has partnered with the Goochland Christmas Mother to support families in need around the holidays. Our practice groups are each assigned a family, and our NOVA families then provide gifts according to the family’s need and wishes.

Short Pump Mile Youth Road Race

Since 2009, NOVA has organized and promoted the largest youth timed road race in Richmond. In 2014, over 1,200 youth participated in the one-mile race and the 400 meter fun run. Each year, race sponsor Bon Secours/St. Mary’s for Kids awards a total of $2,000 to the five schools with the highest number of participants; these prizes support the schools’ physical education programs. Net proceeds from the race support NOVA’s other outreach initiatives.

Scholarships & Financial Aid

NOVA offers financial aid and full scholarships to swimmers as needed. Our financial aid committee evaluates all requests and makes a determination on each one on a rolling basis. In 2013/14 alone, NOVA awarded $30,000 in support for the competitive youth swim team.
