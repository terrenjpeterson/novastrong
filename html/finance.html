A NOVA for the Future

THE FINANCIAL PICTURE

NOVA of Virginia Aquatics is a nonprofit, 501(c)3 organization. Revenues come from dues and fees, grants, fundraising and community support. NOVA's finances are audited each year; the latest statements are publicly available on http://www.giverichmond.org.

NOVA is incredibly fortunate to be in a solid financial position, which will allow the club to embark on this expansion project. NOVA programs generate sufficient cash to cover operating costs and meet the club's financial obligations. NOVA's strong position will allow reinvestment in facilities, programs and staff, and, with the addition of capital campaign funding, expansion. During the recent planning period, NOVA has been able to set aside some funds to support the project.

The cost of the expansion of the NOVA Aquatics Center is currently estimated to be $6.75 - $7 million. NOVA's plan for funding the expansion is threefold: through borrowing, fundraising, and using club surpluses collected over the last few years. NOVA will raise as much of the funds as possible toward the project. Fundraising dollars will come from club families, major partners, philanthropic and corporate foundations, and business and community supporters. We will be able to move forward once a minimum of $2.0 million in gifts and pledges is secured. The balance will be covered by cash on hand and financing.


Over the last two years and to be continued as the project progresses, NOVA has developed revenue streams which will allow the club to meet long-term obligations as well as cover operating costs of the new facility. As the expansion is complete, expansion of current programming and the addition of new programming options will ensure NOVA's long-term financial security.
