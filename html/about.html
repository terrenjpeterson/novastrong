THE NOVA STORY

The NOVA story begins in 1987, with the formation of the nonprofit club practicing in three locations. The name was chosen to reflect the new energy that would come from the founding of a club with national aspirations. Eventually, the three branches split, and NOVA in its current incarnation was born. The club continued to practice in rented space from a number of years. in 1997, the original NOVA Aquatics Center, which includes the current competition pool, was built through the diligent fundraising efforts of the board and swimming families.

In 2005, the second pool was added when the team grew and borrowed money to finance the project. Our current facility includes two 10 lane, 25 yard pools for short course swimming, locker rooms, offices and the Virginia Swim Shop. Since the beginning, NOVA has grown from 90 swimmers to 800. Over time, the organization has also grown not only to serve those interested in our competitive youth swim team, but also adults interested in competitive and recreational swimming, children taking swim lessons, and more. We currently serve almost 600 swim families and teach almost 5,000 swim lessons per year. We have expanded our programs to suit virtually every swimmer's need

Growth over time has allowed NOVA to broaden its programs and fulfill its mission - to develop excellence in swimmers of all ages and abilities by challenging and motivating them to reach their full potential in swimming and in life.
