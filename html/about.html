THE NOVA STORY

NOVA of Virginia Aquatics is a nonprofit 501(c)(3), parent-owned and –directed swim club located in the Richmond, Virginia area. NOVA is a full-service swim club that meets the needs of every level ofswimmer, from lessons to Masters. The club is currently ranked seventh in the nation for overall club performance for swimming competition and organization by USA Swimming. We are recognized as a Gold Medal Club, one of just twenty in the country.
Led by head coach Geoff Brown, USA Swimming’s 2012 Developmental Coach of the Year, NOVA’s coaching staff has led its team to three NCSA Junior National Swimming Championships, won the Virginia Swimming Short Course Age Group Championship each year since 1999, and won the Virginia Swimming Senior Long Course Championship every year since 2006. As a comprehensive aquatics center, NOVA has not only the number one team in Virginia Swimming, but also the region’s leading youth swim lessons program, teaching over 5,000 lessons each year, as well as the top Master’s swimming program in Virginia.

The NOVA story begins in 1987, with the formation of the nonprofit club practicing in three locations. The name was chosen to reflect the new energy that would come from the founding of a club with national aspirations. Eventually, the three branches split, and NOVA in its current incarnation was born. The club continued to practice in rented space from a number of years. In 1997, the original NOVA Aquatics Center, which includes the current competition pool, was built through the diligent fundraising efforts of the board and swimming families. In 2005, the second pool was added when the team grew and borrowed money to finance the project.

Our current facility includes two 10 lane, 25 yard pools for short course swimming, locker rooms, offices and the Virginia Swim Shop. Since the beginning, NOVA has grown from 90 swimmers to 800. Over time, the organization has also grown not only to serve those interested in our competitive youth swim team, but also adults interested in competitive and recreational swimming, children taking swim lessons, and more. We currently serve almost 600 swim families and teach almost 5,000 swim lessons per year. We have expanded our programs to suit virtually every swimmer's need

Growth over time has allowed NOVA to broaden its programs and fulfill its mission - to develop excellence in swimmers of all ages and abilities by challenging and motivating them to reach their full potential in swimming and in life.
